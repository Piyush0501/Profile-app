Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee OM KBE FRS FREng FRSA FBCS, also known as TimBL, is an English computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web.
He is a Professorial Fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Born: 8 June 1955 (age 66 years), London, United Kingdom
Awards: The President's Medal, Millennium Technology Prize, MORE Spouse: Rosemary Leith (m. 2014), Nancy Carlson (m. 1990–2011) Education: The Queen's College (1973–1976),
Emanuel School (1969–1973), Sheen Mount Primary School
Children: Ben Berners-Lee, Alice Berners-Lee Parents: Conway Berners-Lee, Mary Lee Woods
